Public Sector Accounting and Administrative Practices in Nigeria Volume 1

The Nigerian Public Sector consists of three separate levels of government whose powers and relationships are not only defined but are also guaranteed by the constitution. They are the Federal, State and Local Governments. They conduct their activities through two major forms of organisations — Ministries/Extra Ministerial Departments and Parastatals (Public Enterprises). Parastatals include all corporations, authorities, boards, councils and limited liability companies in which the government has either full, majority or controlling interest. Officers in Ministries/Departments are referred to as Civil Servants while those in Parastatals are referred to as Public Officers. What then is Government Accounting? And what is the purpose of keeping the Accounts? Government Accounting can be defined as the process of recording, analysing, classifying, summarising, communicating and interpreting financial information about government in aggregate and in details, reflecting all transactions involving the receipt, transfer and disposition of government funds and property.....

Introduction to the Concept of IPSAS Accrural Accounting

The accounting profession is built on a bedrock of “Rules of Accounting” which guide the book-keeping, recording and preparation of accounts/financial statements and theorize the works of the accounting profession. These rules of accounting are known and referred to as the Concepts of Accounting.
Two of these Accounting Concepts – the Accrual and Matching Concepts, focus on the method of tracking income and expenses in accounting. These oncepts form the basis for categorization of the methods of keeping track of an entity’s income and expenses.
